## Offer Design Notes (internal — don't share with Emilio)

### The Pricing Problem

Emilio anchored at **$500 one-time setup** in the follow-up call. That was for a basic GHL migration (pages + emails + calendar). Since then, the roadmap grew significantly:

- Confirmation Page (research hub with breakout video structure)
- Post-Booking Email Sequence (6 research emails)
- Sales Rep Pre-Call Outreach Protocol
- Breakout video scripts (8 scripts we write, he records)
- Pixel conditioning + Conversion API setup
- WhatsApp multi-flow automation (non-booker + booked + long-term)
- 2-3 day booking window strategy
- Full GHL pipeline with tags, scoring, and routing

**The $500 anchor covers maybe 20% of what we're actually delivering.** We need to reframe the conversation.

### Applying the Conversion Rate Formula

```
CR = (Affordability × Ease × Desire) / Risk
```

- **Desire: VERY HIGH** — 60% no-show rate = Emilio is losing money every single day. His sales reps say when people actually show up, they close well. The problem is crystal clear.
- **Ease: HIGH** — Emilio provides assets (videos, branding), we do literally everything else. His effort is recording 8 short videos from scripts we write.
- **Risk: We must crush this** — see mitigation below.
- **Affordability: The lever to pull** — We don't compete on cheap. We compete on ROI. Frame the price against money he's currently losing to no-shows.

### The ROI Math (use this on the pitch)

Emilio's numbers:
- Course price: $500-$650
- Current show rate: ~40% (60% no-show)
- Target show rate: 65%+ (conservative)
- Assume 20 calls booked per month
- Assume 30% close rate on showed calls

**Current state:**
- 20 booked × 40% show = 8 show up
- 8 × 30% close = 2.4 sales/month
- 2.4 × $575 avg = **$1,380/month revenue**

**After our system:**
- 20 booked × 65% show = 13 show up
- 13 × 30% close = 3.9 sales/month
- 3.9 × $575 avg = **$2,242/month revenue**

**Delta: +$862/month** from show rate improvement alone.

And this is conservative — the post-booking research sequence also makes prospects *more educated*, which should increase close rate from 30% to 35-40%. At 35%:
- 13 × 35% = 4.55 sales → $2,616/month → **+$1,236/month**

**Our full build pays for itself in under 2 months at any reasonable price point.**

## Pitch Strategy

### Frame the Conversation

Don't lead with price. Lead with the ROI math.

**Opening:**
> "Emilio, I mapped out everything we discussed. Before I show you the build plan, let me walk you through the math on your show rate.
>
> You're at about 40% right now. That means for every 20 calls booked, 12 don't show up. At your price point, that's roughly $3,000-$4,000 in lost revenue every month — just from no-shows.
>
> The system I'm proposing doesn't just migrate you to GHL. It builds a research engine between booking and the call that educates prospects, builds trust, and gets them excited to show up."

### Present Tiers as a Choice (not a pitch)

> "I've structured this as two options. Option 1 is the $500 migration we talked about — gets you off ClickFunnels, onto GHL, with a better funnel than today. Option 2 is $1,500 and includes the full Research Hub System that actually attacks the show rate problem — confirmation page, breakout videos, research emails, sales rep protocol, the works.
>
> Both are delivered in 10 days. Both come with a guarantee. You pick whichever makes sense for where you are right now."

### If He Picks Tier 1 (don't fight it)

> "Great. Let's get this live. Once it's running and you see the booking data, we can always layer the Research Hub on top. I'll have everything ready to go."

This respects his budget anchor while leaving the door open. Per the offers skill: "Once you've gotten a customer, you're a lot more motivated to just figure it out." Same applies here — once we're in, the upsell is natural.

### If He Pushes Back on Tier 2 Price

Use the refund math:
> "I hear you. Let me put it this way — if your show rate doesn't improve by 10 points in 30 days, I refund $1,000 of it. You keep the base funnel no matter what. So worst case, you paid $500 for the same thing Option 1 would've given you. Best case, you're making an extra $800-$1,200/month forever."
